Benefits of Passion Fruit

by Excelligence

Posted on November 20, 2018 at 12:00 PM

Introduction

Passion Fruit is the fruit of Passiflora Vine which is a type of passion flower. It is also called as Granadilla, Purple Granadilla, Yellow Passion Fruit. Commonly consumed are two types purple colored and yellow colored.The passion fruit is a vigorous, climbing vine that clings by tendrils to almost any support. It can grow 15 to 20 ft. per year once established and must have strong support. It is generally short-lived (5 to 7 years).

Name Origin

We may think from its name that the fruit got its name from some love story or it is a fruit which evokes passion. the flower of the Passiflora edulis was known by Spanish missionaries as the flower of the five wounds because it illustrated the crucifixion of Christ . Hence, the name passion fruit was originated.

It is native to Brazil,Paraguay and Argentina but is now grown in different tropical and subtropical countries around the world.

It is rich in antioxidants, polyphenols, carotenoids. Antioxidants protect body from free radicals. Antioxidants present includes Vitamin A and C. Polyphenols have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. It is a good source of fiber.

Therapeutic Benefits
Good for Diabetics

It has low glycemic Index and high fiber content. Foods high on the glycemic index like white rice and white bread will break down easily and cause blood sugar and insulin level spikes after meals, which is followed by rapidly dropping blood sugar levels. The sugar from passion fruit is slowly absorbed into the bloodstream, which prevents sugar crashes, sugar cravings, and mood swings.
Enhances skin glow and reverses ageing

As this fruit contains vitamin A, vitamin C and other nutrients vital to skin health. Vitamin A, particularly beta carotene contained is required for healthy skin, cell growth and reproduction. Vitamin C is an antioxidant that reduces damages caused by free radicals, which may cause ageing. thus preventing pre-mature aging and keeping your immune system strong.
Regulates blood pressure

Passion fruit has high potassium content. Potassium relaxes the tension of blood vessels and promotes increased blood flow. This improves cardiovascular health.

Best for enhancing bone health

Since passion fruit is a rich source of many minerals aiding in bone cell growth. It will help in reducing osteoporosis and related ailments.
Enhances digestive health

It is a rich source of fiber. Fiber facilitates healthy digestion and bowel movements.

The pulp, leaves, and flowers of the passion fruit has been long used as a sedative by South American Indians and is noted for its calming effect. In Brazil, the passionflower is also used to make a drink called maracuja grande, which is used to treat throat ailment such as bronchitis and asthma

Eating Style

They are commonly eaten fresh by simply halving the fruit and scooping out the pulp. It is also a popular additive for drinks and each nation has its own unique variation. In South Africa, it is blended with milk while elsewhere it is a popular flavoring for soft drinks or alcoholic beverages (Morton). The fruit can also be made into syrup, which is frequently used as a topping for ice creams and ices
