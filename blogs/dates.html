Facts about dates that you might not know

by Excelligence

Posted on November 30, 2018 at 04:00 PM

History

The word dates originated from Greek daktylos meaning finger or toe due resemblance between the oblong fruit of the date palm and human fingers, but some say it is from the resemblance of the plant's leaves to the palm of the hand.

  • The fruit has been in middle east around 7000 years
  • Date was called as 'tree of life' in the middle east due to its many uses
  • In Judaism, date is regarded as one of the seven holy foods/seeds
  • Date is mentioned in many places in Bible and Quran
  • Shelf life of date are about 1-2 years.

Nutrional Ingredients

An adult can survive only on 15 dates/day without any other food items.

  • Rich in antioxidants called polyphenols,carotenoids
  • Fat free and source of protien
  • Although dates are high in sugar many date varities are have low Glycemic Index
  • It is a high source of energy, as 100 g of flesh can provide an average of 314 kcal
  • Dates contain high levels of selenium, copper, potassium, and magnesium, moderate concen-trations of manganese, iron, phosphorus, and calcium and small quantities of boron The consumption of 100 g of dates can provide over 15% of the recommended daily allowance from these minerals.
  • High in dietary fiber (8.0 g/100 g), insoluble dietary fiber was the major fraction of dietary fiber in dates.
  • Contains wide range of fatty acids

How dates are consumed

Take dried dates and roast nuts in equal proportions and blend them to a smooth paste in a food processor,almonds are good, as are walnuts or hazelnuts. Add some nutmeg or ground cardamom. Form the paste into balls and roll in more chopped nuts, then store in the fridge for half an hour.
